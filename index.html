Introduction

Flutes have always been played in two different positions. Both vertically and horizontally. The flute is the oldest woodwind instrument. The first most likely coming from China. It was an important instrument in courtly music, military signaling, and marching.

There is a real stretch of history when it comes to the history of the flute. In the 1600s-1700s, individual soloists were born. Where they added their own flare, as more registers were added to the flute. Today there are a range of famous flutes ranging from classical to R&B. Here are a list of some of them:

  • James Galway
  • Herbie Mann
  • Alexa Hayes
  • Brian Jackson
  • Gaubert
The Different Types of Flutes

There are many different types of flutes. But people only seem to know 3 types, the standard, piccolo and harmony flutes. Today, many people tend to play flutes that are in the horizontal position starting off with the concert flute. We know now, that the flute is also played transversely.

Let's not be boring people and stick with the original way that people tend to play the flute. Let's expand our repetoire. To do that, here is a list of other flutes:

  • Piccolo
  • Alto Flute
  • Bass Flute
  • Concert Flute
  • Contrabass Flute
  • Bansuri Bamboo Flute
